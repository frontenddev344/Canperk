Canada offers a plethora of off-the-beaten-track experiences for adventurous travelers. These lesser-known activities provide unique and unforgettable adventures. Here are six off-the-beaten-track things to do in Canada:

Hike the West Coast Trail, British Columbia

Located on Vancouver Island, the West Coast Trail is a challenging 75 km (46.6 miles) long backpacking trail that takes hikers through ancient rainforests, rugged coastline, and stunning beaches. It's a fantastic way to immerse yourself in the pristine wilderness of the Pacific Northwest.

view more

Visit Fogo Island, Newfoundland and Labrador

Fogo Island is a remote and culturally rich island off the northeastern coast of Newfoundland. Experience the island's unique art scene, stay in architecturally stunning modern lodges known as "Fogo Island Inns," and explore the dramatic coastal landscapes.

view more

Canoe the Nahanni River, Northwest Territories:

The Nahanni River is a UNESCO World Heritage site and offers a world-class canoeing experience. Paddle through towering canyons, majestic waterfalls, and untouched wilderness in Canada's northern territory.

view more

Explore the Bay of Fundy, New Brunswick/Nova Scotia

The Bay of Fundy is famous for having the highest tides in the world. Experience the dramatic tidal changes, walk on the ocean floor during low tide, and witness the tidal bore—a unique phenomenon where the incoming tide forms a wave upstream.

view more

Discover Haida Gwaii, British Columbia

Formerly known as the Queen Charlotte Islands, Haida Gwaii is an archipelago off the northern coast of British Columbia. It's rich in Indigenous culture, offers incredible wildlife viewing opportunities, and features unique totem poles and ancient Haida village sites.

view more

Sail the Northwest Passage

For the ultimate Arctic adventure, consider a cruise through the Northwest Passage. This legendary sea route in Canada's Arctic allows you to explore remote communities, spot Arctic wildlife, and witness breathtaking ice formations.

view more

Remember that these off-the-beaten-track experiences often require proper planning, permits, and sometimes expert guidance. Always prioritize safety and respect for the environment and local communities while embarking on such adventures.
